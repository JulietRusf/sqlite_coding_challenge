# INSIGHTS

- Jacob Foster is the highest-spending customer, with a total lifetime spend of $8,722.67. This indicates a highly valuable individual customer who could be targeted for retention strategies such as loyalty rewards or exclusive offers.

- The top two customers (Jacob Foster and Ethan Gomez) spend significantly more than the rest of the customer base, suggesting that a small group of customers contributes a disproportionate share of total revenue.

- Electronics is by far the top-performing product category, generating $25,364.23 in revenue. This is roughly double the revenue of the next highest category (Furniture), making it the primary driver of overall sales.

- Furniture is the second strongest category, but there is a steep drop-off after it, indicating reliance on a limited number of categories.

- Grocery and Stationery categories generate minimal revenue, suggesting low demand or an opportunity to reevaluate product strategy.

- There is noticeable salary variation within departments, as multiple employees earn above their department average, indicating differences in experience or performance.

- Tampa has the highest number of Gold-level customers (4), showing a strong concentration of loyal customers in that city.

- Gold customers are not evenly distributed across cities, suggesting geographic differences in customer loyalty.

- These differences present opportunities for targeted marketing, focusing on growing loyalty in weaker cities while maximizing retention in strong ones like Tampa.
